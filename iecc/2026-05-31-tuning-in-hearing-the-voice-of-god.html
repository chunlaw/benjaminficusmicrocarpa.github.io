🌟 Main Takeaways

  1. Hearing God is for every believer — not Mother Teresa, Billy Graham, or "professional Christians" only; my sheep hear his voice.
  2. Relationship comes first — belonging to Christ (the fold) is the starting point before expecting to hear clearly.
  3. God speaks organically — he is not a distant clockmaker; he continues to speak with macro and micro care for your life.
  4. Competing voices are real — strangers, culture, and often your own agenda are the loudest static.
  5. Open ears are a gift and a choice — David prayed God would open his ears (kara); the pierced ear in Exodus 21 pictures voluntary lifelong devotion.
  6. Three stakes — hearing proves you are God's child, protects you from self-made failure, and provides wisdom for true success.
  7. Prayer is two-way — never say "amen" in the sense of ending the conversation; keep the loop open with the Shepherd.

🌏 Acoustic Profile — China, 1991

Brett and Shannon taught English in western China — "professors" with accounting and nursing degrees, accredited mainly as native speakers. Students gravitated to their classes over George and Helen, a credentialed British couple. George grew bitter; the real reason wasn't popularity but acoustic profile.

Languages are best understood within certain frequency ranges. British English sits higher (~12,000 Hz); George was not only British but Scottish — hard for learners to parse. American English was easier for students to "lock onto."

The sermon pivot: What frequency does God speak on? How do we tune our ears, adjust the noise, and focus to hear what he is saying? Jesus says repeatedly, "Whoever has ears, let them hear" — you can have physical ears and still not listen.

👂 Life's Most Desperate Need

Pastor Brett has never heard God's voice audibly. He hears through ideas, nudges, impressions, Scripture, and sometimes through others. Yet hearing from God is not reserved for the super-spiritual — it is the expectation for every believer.

Without hearing God, we construct life on our own — disastrous now and eternally. God reveals salvation and guides how we submit to his loving hand.

  • 1. Proves you are a child of God — "My sheep hear my voice" (John 10). Followers of Christ, not a special subcategory.
  • 2. Protects you from failure — God's ways are good; left alone we make crooked paths (Proverbs 3:6).
  • 3. Provides success in life — "I will counsel you with my loving eye on you" — wisdom and instruction when we come with open hands, not pre-packaged agendas asking only for blessing.

📖 Open Ears — Psalm 40 & the Pierced Ear

📖 Psalm 40:6-8 (NIV, excerpt)
"Sacrifice and offering you did not desire — but my ears you have opened… Here I am… I desire to do your will, my God; your law is within my heart."

David learned God wanted relationship more than ritual performance. The Hebrew word kara means to open — even to excavate or pierce. In Exodus 21, a servant who loves his master and refuses freedom has his ear pierced at the doorpost — a lifelong sign of chosen devotion. David's prayer becomes ours: Lord, open our ears to hear you.

🐑 John 10 — Shepherd, Sheep & the Sheepfold

Jesus uses the sheepfold — rock pens waist-high, shared by multiple shepherds. At night sheep mingle; in the morning each shepherd calls and his sheep follow the voice they know. Strangers they flee.

📻 Four Frequencies — John 10:27

Jesus packs four relational truths into one short verse. Each is a "channel" to tune into as you learn to hear the great Shepherd.

CH 01

My sheep

Belonging first. A possessive love — you are his. The starting point is relationship: sinners who trust Christ, Spirit-indwelt, brought into God's household. Without the fold, there is no promise of hearing.

CH 02

Hear my voice

Divine access. Every believer can grow in hearing — impressions, Scripture, community, circumstances. Not only for the spectacular or charismatic. God wants his children to recognize his voice over strangers and static.

CH 03

I know them

Known completely, loved still. He knows your needs, wounds, selfish bends, and gifts. That knowledge releases white-knuckled control: "God, speak — I am all ears."

CH 04

They follow me

Joyful obedience. Sheep don't renegotiate departure time. Maturity says: your ways are good — instruct me. Trust grows as you see disaster when you follow only yourself.

🎯 Tuning Out Static, Tuning In to God

Hearing God is your most desperate need — not because you are second-class if you don't hear audibly, but because what is available to every Christian is worth pursuing with open ears and open hands.

🔊 Competing Voices

📡 Your voice

Often the loudest: "God, bless my plans." Control first, then ask for approval. Maturity asks: What's your agenda?

📡 Strangers & noise

Culture, fear, guilt, and voices that don't belong to the Shepherd. Sheep flee what they don't know.

📻 The Shepherd

Scripture (primary), Spirit-shaped impressions, godly community, circumstances — trained over time like sheep know their shepherd's call.

👂
Fine-tune daily

Keep prayer two-way — don't "hang up" after presenting requests. Stay in the conversation throughout the day.

📖
Anchor in Scripture

God's Word is the primary frequency. Impressions and circumstances are tested against what God has already spoken.

🙌
Pre-decide obedience

Come trusting his character: "Here I am, Lord, speak" — rather than "Here's my agenda — please sign off."

Still and know: The service closed with worship on Psalm 46:10 — Be still, and know that I am God. Let your appetite be whetted for more of him this series: tune in, walk in freedom, not guilt — as children who seek the Shepherd's guidance.

💭 Reflection & Discussion

📝 From the service

  • How would you respond if asked, "Does God speak to you?" What is a recent example of how God spoke to you? How did you respond?
  • What are some ways in which you can improve your listening skills to the God who longs to connect with you?

🔍 Going deeper

  • Where do you most often ask God to bless decisions you've already made, rather than asking what he wants first?
  • Which of the four frequencies (my sheep / hear / know / follow) is weakest in your walk right now? What would strengthening it look like this week?
  • What "static" — your own voice, culture, busyness — do you need to turn down to hear the Shepherd more clearly?
  • How does the pierced-ear picture in Exodus 21 reshape the way you think about listening to God?
  • Pastor Brett said he hears through impressions, Scripture, and others — never audibly. Does that encourage or unsettle you? Why?

🙏 Prayer prompts

  • Open ears: Lord, excavate and open my ears — I choose lifelong devotion to you as my Master.
  • Less static: Quiet the noise of my agenda; help me hear your voice above my own.
  • Follow: Great Shepherd, I trust that you know me; give me courage to follow even when the path is unclear.

⚡ Challenge for the week

  • Each morning, pray once in each direction: Speak (listen), Know (rest in being known), Follow (one step of obedience you'll take that day). Keep the loop open — don't "amen" your conversation with God for the day.
